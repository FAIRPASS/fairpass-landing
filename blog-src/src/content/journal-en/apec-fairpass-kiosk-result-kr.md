## FAIRPASS Self-Service Kiosks Process 8,000+ Registrations at APEC Energy Ministers' Meeting

The case for automated, self-service registration at large-scale international events just got considerably stronger.

At the **APEC Energy Ministers' Meeting held in Busan, South Korea in August 2025**, FAIRPASS self-service badge kiosks were deployed on-site, processing registrations for **over 8,000 attendees** across the event week. The deployment marks one of the most high-profile public-sector validations of kiosk-based onsite registration technology in the Asia-Pacific MICE calendar to date.

---

## What Is FAIRPASS? One Touch. Instant Badge. No Staff Required.

**FAIRPASS** is a self-service badge issuance kiosk designed for conferences, conventions, and large-scale corporate events. The workflow is straightforward:

1. The attendee approaches the touchscreen kiosk
2. They enter their pre-registered details or scan a QR code
3. Identity is verified instantly
4. A **printed name badge is issued on the spot — in seconds**

For event organizers, the contrast with traditional registration is immediate. Gone are the rows of staff manually sorting through pre-printed badge packets, the alphabetical queues, and the inevitable bottlenecks at venue entrances. FAIRPASS replaces that friction with a self-contained, scalable check-in flow.

Beyond speed, FAIRPASS integrates with **QR-based check-in systems**, giving event operations teams **real-time visibility into attendance data** — a capability increasingly expected at government and enterprise-level events.

## APEC Deployment Triggers 6 New Business Inquiries — Including a 20,000-Attendee Event

The operational success at APEC translated directly into commercial momentum.

Following the event, **six organizations that witnessed FAIRPASS in action on-site reached out to explore deployment for their own events.** Among those, at least one inquiry involves a large-scale event with an expected attendance of **over 20,000 visitors** — a scale that would represent a significant step up in FAIRPASS's operational footprint.

While the revenue contribution from a government-hosted international conference is naturally constrained by procurement structures, the **brand equity gained from APEC is proving to be a powerful sales asset.** Within the Korean MICE industry, the sentiment is clear: *"One line of APEC on your client reference list is worth more than a hundred product demos."*

For B2B event technology vendors globally, this reflects a well-understood dynamic — **anchor clients in high-prestige public-sector events create a halo effect** that accelerates private-sector sales cycles significantly.

## Kiosk-Based Registration Is Becoming Standard in High-Volume International Events

This deployment is not an isolated story. It reflects a broader and accelerating shift across the MICE industry — particularly in Asia-Pacific, where international conferences, governmental summits, and trade exhibitions regularly draw thousands of attendees who expect fast, frictionless entry.

The operational logic is compelling:

- **Speed:** Self-service kiosks dramatically reduce per-attendee check-in time compared to manual badge distribution
- **Accuracy:** On-demand badge printing eliminates errors from pre-printed badge runs
- **Scalability:** Multiple kiosk units can be deployed in parallel, matching throughput to peak arrival windows
- **Data:** Real-time QR check-in integration gives organizers live headcount and session attendance data

The pattern emerging across markets is consistent: **successful deployments at government or multilateral events act as proof points that unlock private-sector adoption.** Procurement managers and PCO agencies who see the technology perform under pressure at a high-stakes event are far more likely to specify it for their own programmes.
