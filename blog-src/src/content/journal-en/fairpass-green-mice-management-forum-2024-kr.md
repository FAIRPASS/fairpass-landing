## Korea's ₩2.7 Trillion MICE Industry Accelerates Its Digital and Green Transition

South Korea's MICE industry — spanning exhibitions, trade fairs, conferences, and corporate events — is valued at approximately **₩2.7 trillion (approx. USD $2 billion)**. As sustainability pressures mount globally, the sector is undergoing a significant dual transformation: going digital and going green simultaneously.

On **March 29**, the **Green MICE Management Forum** brought together **12 leading organizations** driving sustainability in the MICE sector. Participants shared the latest trends across eco-friendly event operations, sustainable materials sourcing, and digital process innovation — all converging on one goal: establishing a new operational standard for responsible event management.

## FAIRPASS Takes Center Stage as an IT-Driven Sustainability Solution

Among the forum's featured exhibitors, **FAIRPASS** drew significant attention as a platform enabling IT-based sustainability for MICE organizers.

FAIRPASS is a **B2B event management platform** that integrates the full event registration workflow — from **online pre-registration through to on-site check-in** — for exhibitions, trade shows, conferences, festivals, and corporate events of all sizes.

Its core capabilities showcased at the forum include:

- **QR Code Check-In**: Attendees scan a pre-issued QR code at a self-service kiosk upon arrival, instantly triggering the automatic printing of their entry ticket or **name badge** — no staff intervention required.
- **Self-Service Ticketing Kiosks**: Each kiosk unit supports issuance of up to **2,000 tickets or badges per event**, and can simultaneously display **video or image-based advertising content**, adding a sponsorship activation layer while improving operational flow at the venue entrance.
- **Real-Time Attendance Tracking**: Through a dedicated access management app, event organizers gain live visibility into **attendee headcount and dwell time**, enabling genuinely data-driven event operations and post-event reporting.

## On-Demand Badge Printing: Reducing the Carbon Footprint of Event Registration

One of FAIRPASS's most compelling green credentials is its **on-demand badge printing model**. Traditional event registration desks routinely pre-print thousands of name badges and materials — the majority of which go unused, contributing to significant paper waste.

FAIRPASS's self-service kiosk system prints **only what is needed, exactly when it is needed**. This eliminates the over-printing that has long plagued large-scale events. To contextualize the scale of the problem: major exhibition venues in Korea alone generate an estimated **3,000 tonnes of event waste annually**. On-demand, kiosk-based badge issuance directly addresses the resource waste embedded in conventional registration workflows — a step that resonates strongly with event organizers in Singapore, Southeast Asia, and globally who are working toward ISO 20121 compliance or net-zero event commitments.

Beyond waste reduction, the kiosk model also reduces the **manpower burden** at registration desks, cutting queue congestion and improving the attendee experience from the moment of arrival.

## Digital Transformation of Event Registration: Setting a New Benchmark for Sustainable Operations

The Green MICE Management Forum made one thing clear: sustainable event management is no longer limited to swapping plastic for recycled materials. **IT-enabled operational efficiency** has become an equally critical pillar of the green event agenda.

FAIRPASS embodies this convergence — delivering **data-driven operations** and **measurable resource reduction** through the digitization of the event registration desk. For PCO agencies, corporate event teams, and academic conference organizers looking to modernize their on-site operations while meeting growing ESG expectations, FAIRPASS offers a scalable, proven infrastructure layer.
