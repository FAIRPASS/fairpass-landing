Walk the floor after any conference ends and you'll see them: hundreds of plastic badge holders discarded on tables, dropped on the floor, or collected in trash bags.

A 500-person conference generates roughly 2 kg of plastic waste from name badges alone. Most of it ends up in landfill — PVC holders and polyester lanyards can't be recycled through standard streams.

This is changing. Here's why paper badges are now the practical, not just principled, choice.

---

## The Scale of the Problem

### Plastic generated per event

| Event Size | Plastic badge holders | Estimated weight |
|------------|-----------------------|-----------------|
| 100 people | 100 units | ~400g |
| 500 people | 500 units | ~2 kg |
| 1,000 people | 1,000 units | ~4 kg |

Almost all of it is single-use. The combination of PVC plastic and polyester fabric makes standard recycling impossible at most facilities.

FAIRPASS has helped eliminate **over 6,840 kg of plastic** across client events — the CO₂ absorption equivalent of planting 1,240+ trees.

---

## What "All-Paper" Actually Means

The FAIRPASS paper badge (페패 / FAIRPASS Badge) replaces every plastic component:

| Component | Plastic version | Paper version |
|-----------|----------------|---------------|
| Badge card | Printed insert in PVC sleeve | Water-resistant FSC paper |
| Lanyard | Polyester fabric cord | Twisted paper cord |
| Connector | Metal or plastic clip | Paper loop |

**No plastic. No metal. 100% recyclable as paper after the event.**

### Patent-registered technology

Two eco-technology patents protect the FAIRPASS paper badge system:
1. All-paper name badge structural design
2. Water-resistant paper treatment process

The water resistance is the non-obvious part. Standard paper fails with any moisture. The FAIRPASS paper badge handles accidental spills, outdoor humidity, and normal handling without degradation.

---

## Durability in Practice

Common concern: *"Will paper badges fall apart during a two-day conference?"*

Real-world results from multi-day events:
- Day 1–2 conferences: No reported failures
- Outdoor morning registration in light rain: Held up without issue
- Networking receptions with drinks: No degradation

The paper cord has been tested to comparable tensile strength as standard polyester lanyards. The weakest point is the connection between cord and card, which is why the paper loop connector was specifically designed and patented.
