If you manage corporate conferences, academic congresses, or government forums, you've probably experienced this:

> "Registration closes in 3 days, 200 emails in the inbox, spending the night sorting spreadsheets."

Registration automation isn't a luxury — it's **infrastructure that determines event quality**. Here's a practical guide to what matters when choosing and implementing an online registration system.

---

## 1. The Hidden Cost of Manual Registration

When you manage registrations via email and spreadsheets, the costs are real but often invisible:

| Task | Manual | Automated |
|------|--------|-----------|
| Confirmation email | Sent manually per registrant | Instant, automatic |
| Duplicate check | Manual review | System-blocked automatically |
| Attendee count | Spreadsheet updates (ad hoc) | Real-time dashboard |
| On-site name list | Sorted the night before | One click |
| Cancellations | Email review + manual edit | Attendee self-service |

**Key insight:** Event managers typically spend 40–60% of their preparation time on registration-related admin. Automation reclaims this time for content, speakers, and on-site experience.

---

## 2. Five Things to Evaluate When Choosing a System

### ① Form customization

Each event needs different data. Beyond name/organization/contact, you may need dietary preferences, session selection, or companion information. Look for:

- Drag-and-drop form builder
- Conditional logic (show different fields based on responses)
- Multi-language form support

### ② Payment integration

For paid events, payment gateway integration is non-negotiable.

- Credit card, bank transfer, and mobile payment support
- Early-bird and group discount configuration
- Automatic receipt and tax invoice generation
- Refund processing workflow

### ③ QR code and on-site connection

Registration and check-in must be connected. If they're separate systems, you recreate manual work at a different point.

> FAIRPASS generates personalized QR codes at registration and connects directly to on-site kiosks for instant scan → check-in → badge printing, all without staff intervention.

### ④ Real-time analytics dashboard

You need to monitor registrations continuously up to event day:

- Hourly registration trends
- Breakdown by organization / role
- Session-specific headcounts
- Pending payments

### ⑤ Automated notifications

Reduce the number of "did you receive my registration?" emails with proper automation:

- Instant confirmation email + QR code on submission
- D-7 and D-1 reminder emails
- Change notifications for schedule updates

---

## 3. Before You Go Live: Critical Checks

### Privacy compliance

When collecting attendee data online, local privacy law compliance is mandatory. Your registration form must include:

- Purpose, categories, and retention period of data collected
- Third-party disclosure consent (if applicable)
- Separate opt-in for marketing communications

FAIRPASS provides a compliant privacy consent template built into the platform.

### Test the full flow yourself

Before opening registrations, complete the entire flow as if you were an attendee: register → pay → receive email → verify QR → check in on a test device. Issues always surface at this stage.
